// INTERVIEW SCRIPTS

Every question, every answer, every closing move — memorize these

CLICK TO EXPAND
"Tell me about yourself."
intro▼

"I'm Jowie Flores, an AI automation specialist based in Naga, Bicol. I build intelligent workflow systems using n8n, Make.com, and Zapier — integrated with AI tools like OpenAI and Gemini — that eliminate manual work and drive measurable ROI. I've built 10+ production systems covering lead qualification, CRM automation, AI agents, and content pipelines. My goal with every client is simple: you give me your most painful manual process, I automate it, and we both measure the results."

⚡ TIP: 60 seconds max. End on the value you deliver. Eye contact on camera. Practice until it's natural, not recited.
"What makes you different from other automation freelancers?"
intro▼

"Most automation freelancers connect apps. I engineer systems that think, qualify, route, and act — by integrating AI agents directly into workflows. I also build RAG systems that train AI on a client's own data so it knows their business inside out. And every workflow I deliver comes with full documentation, video walkthroughs, and error handling built-in — so clients are never dependent on me to manage their own systems."

⚡ TIP: Lead with AI agents + RAG — that combination is rare and premium. Mention documentation to kill the "what if you disappear" fear.
"How long have you been doing this? / Are you experienced?"
intro▼

"I've been building automation and web systems for [X time], and I'm currently doing advanced AI automation training with n8n's ambassador in the Philippines. More importantly — my portfolio at freelancer-jowie.onrender.com has 10 production-grade workflows you can see right now. I'm not learning on your project. I've already built this type of system."

⚡ TIP: If you're new, pivot fast to your portfolio. Clients care about results, not years. Your built work is your proof.
"Why should I hire you over a full-time employee or an agency?"
intro▼

"A full-time automation specialist in the US runs $60k–90k/year plus benefits. An agency adds layers of overhead and account managers between you and the actual builder. I give you senior-level AI automation expertise at a fraction of that cost, with direct communication, faster turnaround, and no overhead. You pay for results, not a seat."

⚡ TIP: Know US salary benchmarks. $60–90k/year = $5–7.5k/month. Your $1,500 project is a 90% discount.
"n8n vs Make vs Zapier — which do you recommend?"
technical▼

"Zapier — easiest to use, great for simple flows, but expensive at scale. Make.com — visual, powerful, great mid-level choice. n8n — my top recommendation. Self-hostable so you own your data, full JavaScript access, native AI agent support, and costs a fraction of Zapier at scale. I use whichever fits the client's stack — but if it's a new build with AI involved, n8n wins every time."

⚡ TIP: Don't trash Zapier if they use it. Suggest n8n for new AI-heavy builds, let simple existing Zapier flows stay.
"Can you build AI agents? What exactly have you built?"
technical▼

"Yes — I build multi-step AI agents in n8n using OpenAI GPT-4 and Gemini. Specifically: an AI SDR that qualifies inbound leads and routes them to CRM, a RAG-based support agent trained on company docs using Supabase vector storage, an AI appointment setter that reads emails and books meetings, and an AI content system that repurposes one video across YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, and Instagram. I understand both the automation layer and the AI layer — most freelancers only know one."

⚡ TIP: RAG is the hottest buzzword right now. Lead with "AI trained on YOUR data" for premium projects.
"How do you handle errors and workflow failures?"
technical▼

"Error handling is built in as standard — not an add-on. Every workflow I ship includes: error-catching nodes, Slack/email alerts for critical failures, retry logic for transient API errors, and a logging layer to Google Sheets or Supabase. I also do 2 weeks of post-launch monitoring. My workflows fail gracefully — never silently."

⚡ TIP: This question is really about trust. Clients fear paying for something that breaks and nobody knows. Reassure with specifics.
"How do you handle data security and API credentials?"
technical▼

"All credentials are stored in environment variables or the platform's built-in credential vault — never hardcoded. For n8n, I recommend self-hosted deployment on the client's own VPS so they retain full data ownership. I request only minimum API scopes, sign NDAs on request, and deliver a complete handover doc listing every credential and access point."

⚡ TIP: Have an NDA template ready to send in 5 minutes. See Templates section.
ASK CLIENT: "What's the most painful manual process in your workflow right now?"
you ask this▼

This is your most powerful question. Say it, then shut up and let them talk. Don't interrupt. The more they explain their pain, the more they sell themselves. Your job right now is to listen and take visible notes — you'll mirror their exact words back in your proposal.

⚡ FOLLOW UPS: "How long has this been going on?" / "How many hours per week does it cost your team?" / "What have you tried before?" / "What happens if this isn't fixed in the next 60 days?"
ASK CLIENT: "What tools are you currently using? Walk me through a typical workflow."
you ask this▼

This reveals their entire tech stack, integration complexity, and whether they have any existing automation. Map their stack on paper as they talk — you'll reference it in Section 4 of your proposal.

🚩 RED FLAGS: "We use a custom internal tool" (complex) | "We're not sure" (unclear decision-maker) | "Our IT team controls access" (long approval cycles)
ASK CLIENT: "Have you budgeted for this? What range are you working with?"
you ask this▼

Ask this directly. If they say "$100" for a $2,000 project, better to know now. If they push back: "I ask because I want to propose something realistic. Projects like this typically run $500–3,000 depending on scope. Does that align with your expectations?"

🚩 RED FLAG: "We'll pay based on results" or "Just a small budget" = run, or take a tiny paid discovery sprint first.
"Your rate is too high. Can you do it cheaper?"
rate▼

"I can work within a tighter budget, but I'd need to reduce scope. Let me show you two options: a lean version that solves your core bottleneck for [lower price], and the full system at my standard rate. The full version pays for itself faster — but you choose what fits right now."

⚡ RULE: NEVER just cut your price. Always reduce scope when you reduce price. This protects your value.
"We found someone cheaper on Fiverr."
rate▼

"That's a fair point. The reality is budget automation often breaks in production, lacks error handling, and ships with zero documentation. I've been called in to fix Fiverr automations more times than I can count. Would you want to start with a small paid discovery sprint so you can see exactly how I work before committing?"

⚡ TIP: Don't badmouth Fiverr directly. Offer the discovery sprint as a low-risk entry point for skeptical clients.
"What are your payment terms?"
rate▼

"For new clients: 50% deposit before I start, 50% on delivery and approval. For retainer clients after our first successful project: monthly invoice due by the 5th. I accept bank transfer, Wise, PayPal, or Upwork's payment protection. I send a contract before starting any project — this protects both of us."

⚡ RULE: ALWAYS take a deposit. It filters non-serious clients. Never start work without at least 50% paid — no exceptions.
"Let me think about it."
objection▼

"Of course — this is a real investment. Quick question before you go: is there anything that's still unclear or holding you back? I'd rather address it now than send you off with an unanswered question." [Then shut up and listen. Don't fill the silence.]

⚡ TIP: "Let me think about it" usually means "I have an unaddressed concern." Dig for it.
"We're not ready yet / maybe next quarter."
objection▼

"I hear you — timing matters. Quick question: what would need to happen for you to be ready? Because every month this isn't automated is costing you approximately [X hours × salary = $Y] in avoidable labor. Is there a smaller first step — a $150 discovery audit — that I could do now so you hit the ground running next quarter?"

⚡ TIP: Calculate their monthly cost of the problem in real $ and say it out loud. The discovery sprint is your foot-in-the-door move.
THE CLOSE: After presenting your proposal on a call
closing script▼

"Based on everything we've discussed, I'm confident I can deliver this and the results will be measurable from day one. I can put a full proposal over in the next 24 hours — or if you want to move fast, we can do a small discovery sprint: $150, I audit your current workflows, and give you a full automation roadmap. Most clients just say 'build the whole thing' after seeing that roadmap. Which direction feels right for you?"

⚡ GOLDEN RULE: After asking the close — STOP TALKING. The first person to speak loses. Let the silence sit. This single habit will make you more money than any script.
FOLLOW-UP EMAIL: After the call, within 2 hours
closing script▼

"Hi [Name] — great talking with you! Quick summary of what we covered: [1-2 sentences of their pain point in their words]. [1 sentence of what you'd build]. I'll have the full proposal over by [specific day/time]. Looking forward to it — Jowie"

⚡ TIP: Short. Fast. Specific. Don't summarize the whole call. Goal is to stay top of mind and show you listened.
